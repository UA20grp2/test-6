Please Be Seated

Paul Cocksedge is an English artist based in London. He is best known for blurring the fine line between art and function. 'Please Be Seated' is one of his works. This now well-known work can be found in Finsbury Avenue Square in London and has been built with the aim of embracing and answering the ever-changing rhythm of London's inhabitants. The design allows people to sit on the sculpture, but also to walk underneath it. The sculpture includes three centrally arranged rings made out of pine wood scaffolding planks. The artwork has been placed in one of London's most pedestrian-visited squares and as Cocksedge said himself: “It walks the line between a craft object and a design solution, It occupies the square without blocking it.” This is because he made use of the undulating structure, which means that the openness of the square has remained very well intact.

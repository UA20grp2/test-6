Process

13 november 2020

First brainstorm: Covid-19

In this brainstorm you can see a clear difference between the positive and negative impact corona has on our lives.

The corona crisis has a big impact on the lives of our youth. They feel frustrated and misunderstood. They have a need to be heard. That is why we would like to focus our project on the ‘cry for help’ from our youth.
 

15 november 2020

Looking for a space

We especially wanted to find a space that has a strong connection to our youth. During our search we found multiple spaces that were suitable for our project. These options were an auditorium in a school, a student flat and a gathering space for a local Scout troop. We eventually chose the headquarters for the Scout troop ‘Sint-Jobertus’ that was located in Brasschaat, Antwerp. Our team members Dagmar and Kato themselves are members of this particular Scout troop. Also every single member of our group had in one way or another a connection with some kind of youth movement, and this created an emotional connection with our choice.
Scouts headquarters normally are a place that’s full of action, adventure and fun and this is entirely different from what it is like to be a scout during the pandemic today. Scouts headquarters now are a sad and empty place that is lacking of fun and laughter. The room requires people to be present and when no one is there, the room can’t fulfill its purpose. It desires presence of people. 

17 november 2020

Second brainstorm: Covid-19

After capturing our target group, we started thinking about the connection between the youth and the corona pandemic. We then divided all our findings into a few categories. With this we started to work further and start thinking about how we can start to portray this feeling in our installation.

18 november 2020

Collage Hans Op de Beeck

During our weekly presentation where we presented our location, our coach made a connection with some of the artwork created by Belgian visual artist Hans Op de Beeck. This gave us the idea and inspiration to examine his work and make a collage of it as a form of synthesis of his work that reflects the same feeling we wanted to accomplish with our project. 

18 november 2020

The impact Corona has on us

We also went looking for images that reflected the emptiness and loneliness that we wanted to create. We found images that were a little bit too dramatic, but the collage did give us more insight in what we wanted to accomplish with our project. The right judgement call between positivity and negativity is really important to give the right message. 

19 november 2020

Third brainstorm: Installation

For the brainstorm about the installation we all made a mind map with words. From here on we each searched the web for images that went together with what we came up with. By doing this we all got good ideas and insights.  

21 november 2020

The image

This collage refers to the abandoned Scouts headquarters. Our intention was that they should express the same feeling and ambiance. It’s obvious the people were once present, but not anymore and that results in a feeling of emptiness and alienation. 

21 november 2020

Location

For our location we chose public spaces. That way our project is accessible for our wide target audience. That way, by-passers have the choice whether or not to enter the space. Some of our ideas were the FOMU in Antwerp, the Meir, a forest, a park, the Belgian coastline... 

21 november 2020

Installation room

For this collage we looked for spaces that we could place our installation in. We looked at it from different angles and we eventually chose the space that we considered best for our design.

21 november 2020

Techniques

Our starting point are the pictures we took from the abandoned Scouts headquarters. We want to show these pictures as settings in our space.
There are a few ways we can realize this.

21 november 2020

Display of the human being

In this moodboard we experimented with different ways on how we can show the presence of people in an abstract way. This varies from shadows to white noise and simplistic ways of presence. We wanted to create the feeling that someone was present in the room in a not so obvious way.  

21 november 2020

Display of the human actions

Human interaction and objects show the space was once in use. This leaves marks. You can clearly see the difference between a snapshot and the pictures we took later on. Our goal was to show the ambiance and togetherness in those pictures.

Turbo Installation

Baptiste Debombourg's projects are always related to human aspects such as: our mistakes, doubts, desires and visions of certain realities. He is constantly looking for a relationship between object and space, reality and ideology. "I get my inspirations from everyday life and more specifically from objects that condition. My reflection extends to their use and the behavior they engage in, as well as the emotional relationship we have with them." he says. The turbo was made between 2007 and 2013 and is on display at Galerie HO in Marseille and Maison Martin Margiela in Paris. It is an installation made of white laminated chipboard with the meaning of recovering lost energy. It gives an engine more air. It is a type of auxiliary device that is attached to the combustion engine and is intended to increase the pressure of the permitted gases in order to better fill the cylinders with air. This gives the engine more oxygen and can burn more fuel completely. The result: improved engine performance with a constant cylinder capacity. The turbo expresses the will-to-power phenomenon. By questioning chauvinistic overbinning, "turbo" endangers this representation of male power and the elements that underlie it: will to surpass itself, seek perfection, authority in power, through material representations.

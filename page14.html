You Sit, You Die

Iván Navarro is an artist born in Chile (1972) who gained fame for his works with light, mirrors and glowing tubes with which he makes socially and politically-relevant sculptures and installations. Navarro argues that as a resident of Chile, the political situation in his country has influenced his works, his use in medium and also the meaning and philosophy that is hidden in his realizations. One of his works 'You Sit, You Die' is a good example of this. The work includes a lounge seat made with white fluorescent lamps, Navarro’s representation of the electric chair. Electricity was one of the preferred means used by the Chilean government to aid the administration of corporal punishment. Furthermore, there is also a second facet of criticism in this piece. On the paper seat of the chair, the names of each individual sentenced to death by the State of Florida has been written. Florida was elected here because it was the leader in the highest number of death sentences carried out.

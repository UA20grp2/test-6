Origin project name

Certain rituals and traditions are typical for a scout group. Every member gets their own nickname, they use a certain salute and their uniform isn't complete without the beige shirt with badges. On summer camp there are some more traditions like singing the evening song every night in a big circle before everyone goes to bed. All these rituals and traditions enhance the feeling of togetherness, something that’s difficult to experience nowadays.
The original version of the evening song was sung for the first time in 1919 and was called “Cantique des Patrouilles”, which literally means ‘the song of the patrols’. It was sung by the Jesuit Jacques Sevin, one of the founders of scouting in France. We decided to use this title and mix it up so it can be used as the title for our design. This also improves the feeling of being together in all of this.   

The evening song

O Heer, d'avond is neergekomen,
de zonne zonk, het duister klom.
De winden doorruisen de bomen
en verre sterren staan alom...
Wij knielen neer om u te zingen
in 't slapend woud ons avondlied.
Wij danken u voor wat we ontvingen,
en vragen, heer, verlaat ons niet!

Knielen, knielen, knielen wij neder,
door de stilte weerklinkt onze beê
Luist'rend fluist'ren kruinen mee
en sterren staren teder.
Geef ons Heer, zegen en rust en vreê.

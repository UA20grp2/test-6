The Rainbow Room

Pierre le Riche is an artist in Cape Town, South Africa. He mainly works with sculptures and installations made of ironing and textile work. His design "the Rainbow room" is an installation about homosexuality. The installation takes a critical look at African society and how they feel about homosexuality. It reflected the experience of the artist who grew up as a gay man in conservative South Africa. The design is made up of acrylic yarn in the colors of the gay pride flag. The installation represented a traditional African family living room in the middle of the final of the rugby world cup in 1995. This was a significant point in South Africa's history and one of the last chances the African man had to prove his superiority. Pierre le Riche tries to contrast the concept of homosexuality and masculinity by dressing traditional furniture and more than 150 rugby balls with colourful yarn. In doing so, he tries to question the acceptance of homosexual relationships.

Hubris Atë Nemesis

For the CMCA or the Center for Maine Contemporary Art, Wade Kavanaugh and Stephen B Nguyen have designed an installation that takes on the coastal landscape of Maine, the rough water and the wind. These are represented by long, curved wooden strips that are layered on the floor and ceiling. For this installation, the artists explored the interplay of natural forces, people and the built environment. By making the footpath and the installation so mixed up, the visitor gets the feeling that he is part of the installation himself. The name of the project: 'Hubris Atë Nemesis' is a meeting of 3 Greek words that can be found Greek tragedies. As the designers themselves quote: "Hubris describes arrogant confidence, which transforms to Atë, a ruinous folly or madness, then onto Nemesis, a force of retribution that resets the natural order".

Skyscraper

Studio KCA is an international design firm in Brooklyn, New York that focuses on installation art. At the head of the agency are Jason Klimoski and Lesley Chang. One of their works is 'Skyscraper' or 'The Bruges Whale'. The work was made for the Triennial of Bruges in 2018. Studio KCA chose the shape of a whale or more specifically a blue whale, because this animal is threatened with extinction. Furthermore, it was made to raise attention to the immense amount of plastic waste floating around in our oceans. The base of the structure is an iron skeleton with about 5 tons of plastic found off the coast of Hawaii. This is only a fraction of the 8 million tonnes currently in it. The whole idea behind the whale is: "if a plastic cup can travel such a long way from the shop to the middle of the ocean, then a whale swimming through the canals of Bruges is not that surprising." Finally, a Kickstarter has been created to finance the transport of the whale. The whale after Bruges also stood in Utrecht and made a tour of Asia.
